### Project Overview
- This project provides a comprehensive analysis of retail sales data to support data-driven decisions across staffing, inventory planning, marketing strategy, and operational efficiency. The analysis focuses on understanding sales patterns, product performance, store comparisons, and customer purchasing behavior to uncover opportunities for optimization and business growth.

 ### Objectives
- Identifying days and hours with peak sales to optimize staffing and inventory levels.
- Determining best-selling products by category, type, and detail to guide marketing and inventory decisions.
- Comparing sales across store locations to identify strengths, weaknesses, and potential expansion opportunities.
- Analyzing sales trends by product category to detect growth or decline patterns.
- Identifying seasonal fluctuations to improve demand forecasting.
- Estimating average revenue per transaction to better understand customer spending behavior.

 ### Tools Used
- Power BI – for data modeling, interactive visuals, dashboards, and trend analysis.
- Excel - ( Power Query Editor ) for data cleaning
- DAX – for calculated measures and advanced analytics within Power BI.

 ### Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- The EDA process included the following steps:
- Data Cleaning: Handling missing values, standardizing product categories, and ensuring date/time formats were consistent.
- Time-Series Exploration: Breakdown of sales by day, hour, week, and season.
- Product Analysis: Ranking products based on sales volume, revenue, and category performance.
- Location Analysis: Comparing total sales, average transaction value, and customer volume across locations.
- Trend Detection: Evaluating short-term and long-term shifts in purchasing patterns.
- Revenue Metrics: Calculating revenue per transaction and other KPIs.

 ### Findings
#### Peak Sales Periods
- Specific days and hours showed significantly higher sales, indicating clear peak operational periods where staffing and inventory should be increased.
#### Product Performance
- Certain categories and product types consistently dominated sales.
- Best-selling items were identified, helping highlight high-demand and high-margin products.
 #### Store Comparison
- Strong differences in performance across locations were observed, revealing top-performing stores and those requiring strategic support or restructuring.
 #### Category Trends
- Some product categories showed steady growth, while others indicated declining popularity, signaling potential optimization areas.
#### Seasonal Fluctuations
- Seasonal trends in customer demand were detected, emphasizing the importance of seasonal inventory planning.
#### Revenue Per Transaction
- Calculated average revenue per transaction revealed insights into customer spending behavior and potential upsell opportunities.
 #### Recommendations
- Optimize staffing schedules according to peak sales hours to improve service quality and reduce operational stress.
- Increase inventory for best-selling products, while reevaluating low-performing categories.
- Invest marketing efforts in high-growth product categories and promote underperforming ones strategically.
- Support weaker store locations with targeted strategies, such as local promotions or product assortment adjustments.
- Plan inventory and campaigns around seasonal trends, ensuring adequate stock during high-demand periods.
- Boost average transaction value through bundle offers, cross-selling, and targeted promotions.

- #### Conclusion
- This sales analysis provides valuable insights into customer behavior, operational performance, and product demand across different dimensions. Using Power BI enabled the creation of interactive dashboards that support actionable decision-making. By leveraging these insights, businesses can optimize staffing, improve inventory efficiency, strengthen marketing strategies, and enhance overall profitability.
